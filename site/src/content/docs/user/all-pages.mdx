## What is the All Pages view?

All Pages lists every non-journal page in your graph, sorted alphabetically by title. It is the
fastest way to navigate to a page when you know its name — or close to it — and do not want to
type a search query.

This view gives you a bird's-eye view of your graph's page inventory. Over time it is useful for
identifying orphan pages (pages with no links pointing to them), spotting naming inconsistencies
(`ProductRequirements` vs. `Product Requirements`), and auditing what you have captured.

Journal entries (stored in `journals/`) are excluded from All Pages — use the
[Daily Notes](./daily-notes) view to browse them chronologically.

## How to access it

Tap or click **All Pages** in the sidebar. The view loads immediately with pages sorted A–Z.

## Key interactions

- Tap or click any page name to open it in the main content area
- Scroll to browse all pages alphabetically
- The sidebar stays visible on desktop while reading a page, so you can jump between pages quickly
- Newly created or renamed pages appear in the correct alphabetical position immediately

## What is shown

All pages in the `pages/` directory of the open graph are listed. This includes:

- Pages you created manually
- Pages that were auto-created when you typed a `[[wikilink]]` to a page that did not exist yet
- Pages imported from another graph

Journal entries (`journals/YYYY_MM_DD.md`) are intentionally excluded. Use the Journals sidebar
icon to navigate those.

## Tips

- Use All Pages to audit orphan pages — pages that have no incoming wikilinks are easy to spot
- Alphabetical browsing is faster than search when you know the first letter of a page name
- Combine with [Search](./search) for content-based discovery when you only remember a word from
  the page body rather than its title

## Keyboard shortcuts

All Pages is a browsing view. No keyboard shortcuts are specific to this screen.

## Related features

- [Search](./search) — find pages by keyword when you only remember part of the content
- [Daily Notes](./daily-notes) — browse journal entries chronologically
- [Backlinks](./backlinks) — see which pages link to the page you are currently viewing
