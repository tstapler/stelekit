- Spent time today experimenting with `[[wiki links]]` between pages.
	- Created a new page called "Reading List" and linked it from today's journal entry.
	- The link appeared immediately in the suggestions dropdown after I typed `[[R`.
	- Clicking the link navigated to the new page in one tap.
- Discovered the backlinks panel while on the Reading List page.
	- The sidebar showed "Referenced in: 2026-04-09" with the exact block that linked here.
	- This is the killer feature — I never have to manually maintain a "pages that reference this" list.
	- Read [[Page Linking]] to understand the full mechanics, then [[Backlinks]] for the receiving side.
- A question I had: *How do I see all pages that link here?*
	- Answer: look at the References Panel in the sidebar. Every inbound link appears there automatically.
	- The panel shows the linking page name and a preview of the block containing the link.
- **Observations on the linking model**
	- Creating a link is a side effect of writing. It does not feel like extra work.
	- The graph grows naturally as I connect ideas rather than filing them into folders.
	- A page with many backlinks becomes a hub — worth revisiting and refining.
