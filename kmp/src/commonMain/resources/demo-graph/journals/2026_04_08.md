- First time opening SteleKit today. The interface is clean — just a sidebar, a content area, and an empty block waiting for input.
	- The block-based editing model is immediately different from every other note-taking app I have used.
	- In Word or Notion, I write paragraphs. Here, every sentence can become its own node in a tree.
- Spent an hour reading [[Block Editing]]. A few things clicked:
	- Blocks are not just visual. They are the unit of linking, querying, and reviewing.
	- Nesting with Tab feels more natural than I expected.
	- You can collapse subtrees — this changes how I think about large topics.
- **First impressions**
	- The outliner is faster to navigate than a prose document once you get used to it.
	- Backlinks are automatic — I did not have to do anything to get them.
	- The `[[` shortcut for wiki links is clever; the suggestion dropdown appeared immediately.
	- Journal entries as daily pages is a much better habit-loop than a dedicated "daily notes" app.
- One confusion: I expected Enter to create a paragraph break. It creates a new block. That is the right behavior once you understand the model, but it is a shift.
- Plan for tomorrow: explore page linking and see how the graph builds up.
