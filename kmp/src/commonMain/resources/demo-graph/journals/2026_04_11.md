- First flashcard session today. Read [[Flashcards]] to understand how to set it up, then created a small deck from my spaced-repetition research notes.
- **Creating the first flashcard**
	- Opened the "Research: Spaced Repetition" page.
	- Found a block: "The forgetting curve shows retention drops sharply within 24 hours without review."
	- Added a child block: `card:: true`
	- The block immediately entered the review queue — no separate import step needed.
- **Reviewing the queue**
	- Opened the Flashcards screen from the sidebar.
	- The card appeared with the block text as the prompt.
	- Pressed Space to reveal — in this case the full block is both prompt and answer, so I was testing recall of the key fact.
	- Rated myself "Good" and the next review was scheduled automatically.
- **Notes on spaced repetition**
	- The value is not in the first review — it is in the second, third, and tenth review at the right intervals.
	- Short, atomic cards work better than long blocks. One fact per card.
	- The best time to create a flashcard is immediately after learning something, while the context is fresh.
