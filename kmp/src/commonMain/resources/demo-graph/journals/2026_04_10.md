- Properties experiment today — read [[Properties]] end to end and then tried it on a research page.
- Created a page called "Research: Spaced Repetition" and added page-level properties at the top:
	- author:: Various
	- status:: in-progress
	- topic:: learning science
	- started:: 2026-04-10
- Then added block-level properties to individual note blocks:
	- A block summarising a paper got `source:: Ebbinghaus 1885` and `confidence:: high` as child properties.
	- A block with an open question got `status:: unresolved`.
- **What properties enable**
	- Properties turn a freeform note into a structured record.
	- You can scan a page and immediately know its status, author, and topic without reading the full content.
	- In the future, graph-wide queries can filter pages by property values.
- Key insight: properties in SteleKit are just text with a `key:: value` pattern. There is no schema to configure. You invent your own vocabulary and stay consistent.
	- This is more flexible than a database but less rigorous. The trade-off feels right for personal notes.
- One thing to keep in mind: `Author::` and `author::` are different keys. Settle on lowercase conventions early.
